Rafa Benitez to leave Newcastle if team is not competitive as he eyes another Champions League title

Publish date: 2024-03-14

RAFA BENITEZ has warned Newcastle he would rather leave than manage an uncompetitive team.

And the Spaniard believes he still has it in him to win another Champions League before he retires aged 70.

The Toon are trying to tie down the former Liverpool and Valencia boss, whose contract expires in June, and will give him £100million — plus cash from sales — to spend over two years.

But Benitez clearly feels that is not enough to get the club to where he thinks they should be, which is just outside the top six.

The St James’ Park boss, who turned 59 on Tuesday, said: “What I want is to get a job where I can enjoy competing.

We need a team that can compete in the Premier League, not just survive. It has to be something that makes me, the fans and everybody sure we can compete.

Rafa Benitez

“If I cannot, maybe I have to stay at home and wait.

“I have been here for more than three years and I was clear from day one — we need a team that can compete in the Premier League, not just survive. It has to be something that makes me, the fans and everybody sure we can compete.

“It is not a case of thinking, ‘Maybe we can finish tenth or maybe we can finish 17th’.

“It has to be a team that when you play against anyone, you are sure you can win — not a draw will be a great result.

“We are in a situation where we have to decide if we want to achieve something more.”

Toon managing director Lee Charnley made it clear this week Benitez’s budget is based on the cash left over once costs are taken away from turnover — around £50m a year.

But Benitez said: “If you want to compete against teams between seventh and 12th, how much money have they spent ...£100m all of them.

“I don’t say you have to spend £200m but you have to do things in a way where you can compete in the market.

“Our players, their families, are happy in Newcastle, even better than in London.

“But to sell that, you have to add something more — sometimes it’s wages, sometimes it’s a transfer fee.”

Benitez’s preference is to stay working in England, at least for the next two years while his youngest daughter Agata, 16, finishes school.

But he admits he cannot see himself finishing his career without another crack at the Champions League, which he won with Liverpool in 2005.

Benitez said: “I want eleven years as a manager.

“My wife is fine. For her, it is a relief to stay in England.

“One of my daughters is at uni and the other will go to uni - and that will be the time when we have to sit down and decide.

“But I would like to compete for trophies and the best trophy at the moment is obviously the Champions League.

“So I would like to have the possibility to win the Champions League again in the next 11 years.”

Newcastle United News

Right now, however, Benitez is only concerned about Newcastle’s clash with Southampton as he insists his side are still not safe.

The Toon are seven points clear of third-bottom Cardiff but there are 12 points still to play for.

And Benitez added: “I don't think the job is done.

“I said 38 points would be enough a long time ago, but Cardiff won a couple of games and that changed everything.

“Now it is even more important for us to win another game and be sure everything is fine - then we can think about how high we can go.”
