Young mum, 28, found hanged after 'suffering from visions that her baby had been thrown out of a win

Publish date: 2024-03-04

A YOUNG mum was found hanged after suffering from visions that her baby had been thrown out of a window and run over, a inquest has heard.

Tragic Rachel Morgan was described as a “considerate” and “confident” before the arrival of her second baby.

But the 28-year-old was said to have become anxious and detached and told her mum she was having disturbing visions of her newborn baby being run over, The Manchester Evening News reports.

Jill Robinson, Rachel’s mum, told an inquest: “She was agitated and she was anxious.

“It was in the morning she told me she was not coping. She said was getting visions and that she wanted to speak to someone so I took her to the Royal Bolton Hospital.”

She added: “She was in control of her life. She was considerate, fair, confident and had a great sense of humour.

“She had a great future in front of her.”

Rachel asked to referred to a specialist mother and baby unit, a court heard.

The inquest into her death in Stockport heard Rachel went to the maternity unit where she was told she could not be seen because she had been discharged some weeks before.

She then went to her local GP but Ms Robinson said: “The doctor said ‘you don’t want to go down that route’ in reference to mental health care.

“She was concerned she was going to be labelled mad.”

Rachel was given medication but made several suicide attempts in the months leading up to her death.

She was said to have spent time on suicide forums but according to her mum and partner all she really wanted to referred to a special mother and baby unit.

The court heard the move was blocked by social workers.

In April last year she was sectioned and was found hanged in her hospital room on Saturday, April 16. She died later that month on April 26.

The inquest continues.
