A MUM has revealed how she managed to overhaul her cracked sofa for £20 – and all she needed was a paintbrush and some hard work.

Leather sofas can dull and crack over the years, with traditional cleaning products not restoring them to their former glory.

But one mum decided to completely transform her sofa by painting it, and it only cost £20.

She managed to hide all the cracks and scratches on the cream three-seater, and it looks as good as new.

Sharing incredible before-and-after photos to Facebook group Extreme Budget DIY & Life Hacks, she said: “So a couple of weeks ago I plucked up the courage to paint my sofa.

“So glad I did it it’s like a new sofa for under £20!

“Hidden all scratches from my cat and feels amazing!”

She explained her method for anyone inspired to give their couch a lick of paint, saying: “Before I painted it I cleaned it thoroughly with sugar soap, dried then applied 2 thin coats with a small roller and a small brush for the awkward areas.

“Highly recommend and so far so good after sitting on it no cracks.

“The paint has a wax in it so no need to wax after applying the paint.

“So easy and looks new again!”

The specialist paint is sold online by Frenchic Furniture Paint, and prices start £6.95 and come in a range of colours, with the mum using Wolf Whistle on hers.

While she was able to sit on the sofa the same day, she recommended leaving it for as long as possible.

She added: “Drying time- I was able to sit on mine about 4 hours later as it dries quite quick.
