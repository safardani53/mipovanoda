Mum opens a boutique hotel for sausage dogs, offering luxurious spa treatments, puppachinos an

Publish date: 2024-01-31

A MUM-of-three who desperate to escape the daily grind has opened a luxury boutique hotel like no other - as it's specifically for sausage dogs.

Hannah Stuchfield, 41, from Derbyshire, opened The Sausage Dog Sanctuary, where wiener dogs can be pampered with spa treatments, afternoon tea and countryside walks.

She explained: "I got to the point where I'd had enough of the rat race and I was sick of the nine to five office thing - I wanted to do something a little bit more fun and lighthearted."

Not long after coming up with the idea of the Sausage Dog Hotel, having told her husband of her new dream, she left her job in the e-commerce sector.

The sanctuary was opened in February, and dogs can stay overnight for just £28, with treatments and special experiences totted up to the total.

Unlike even the most lavish kennels, which also offer daycare and overnight boarding, the dachshund hotel offers cupcake afternoon teas complete with 'puppachinos'.

Pooches can also be booked in for countryside walks with treat-based games and exciting puzzles along the way, as well as naps in the pillow-strewn living room.

Extra pampering including nail trimming, teeth cleaning and coat conditioning is also available for those sausage dogs looking for something special.

And as the breed is known for having back problems, Hannah offers Sausage Dog Agility programmes to prevent future health problems.

The retreat is already popular among owners, receiving around 100 bookings for June alone, and coming from as far as Wales and Suffolk.

"They're very en vogue," Hannah explained of the short-legged dog breed. "And what's more, people seem to collect them. I have two myself.

"We offer an alternative to kennels. If someone wants to go on holiday people usually have to take their dog to kennels but we are offering home boarding.

"When you leave your dog in the home with us, they are allowed on the sofas, they are not locked in a kennel all day. They have human interaction."

Hannah, who dubs herself the 'crazy Sausage Dog person', can keep up to 15 dogs at a time - with husband Ewan, 38, helping out when things get busy.

"We really wanted to get away from the kennel thing, aiming instead for a more boutique hotel feel," Hannah continued.

"The Sanctuary reflects what these dogs mean to their owners. You'll get some of them dropping off their dogs in floods of tears like they're dropping their child for the first day of nursery.

"But even the most stressed dog will soon settle in and you'll even get a postcard from them. We spend time to make sure dogs we have are integrated well.

"Everyone who contacts me is fully screened over the phone and when they come to the house, they are able to meet my dogs."
