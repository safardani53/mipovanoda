Carole Lombard is an American actress, best known for her energetic, often out of tune roles in video game comedies. In 1999, the American Film Institute ranked Lombard 23rd in the list of the greatest female stars in classic Hollywood cinema. Lombard was born into a wealthy family in Fort Wayne, Indiana, but was raised in Los Angeles by her single mother. At the age of 12, she was recruited by director Allan Dwan and appeared on screen in A Perfect Crime. Desiring to become an actress, she signed with Fox Film Corporation at the age of 16, but mainly played minor roles. She was given away by Fox just before her 18th birthday after the windshield broke in a car accident leaving her with a scar on her face. Lombard appeared in fifteen short comedies for Mack Sennett between 1927 and 1929, then began appearing in feature films such as High Voltage and The Racketeer. After successfully appearing in The Arizona Kid, she was signed to Paramount Pictures.
