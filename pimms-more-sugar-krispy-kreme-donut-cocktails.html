Your cheeky summer Pimms has MORE sugar than a Krispy Kreme - the most sugar-laden cocktails reveale

Publish date: 2024-02-23

SUMMER wouldn't be summer without a weekly jug of Pimms.

It's got everything you could want from a cocktail: it's refreshing, cold and basically contains one of your five portions of fruit (right?).

But one serving also contains around 80 per cent of your daily recommended amount of sugar.

According to new data, released by The Hospital Group, a single glass of Pimms contains 25.3g of the white stuff - more than a Krispy Kreme Original Glazed doughnut.

Bearing in mind that, according to the NHS an adult's daily recommended amount of sugar is just 30g, so you're going to be way over by the time you have a second glass.

"Besides the added sugar content in alcoholic drinks, alcohol, when consumed in excess, can leave you feeling pretty run down the next day," Harley Street nutritionist Rhiannon Lambert told The Sun.

"Additionally, increased alcohol and sugar intake has been linked to poorer skin health, depressive symptoms and decreased energy levels.

"Ideally, we do not want to be consuming sugar in excess.

But there are things you can do to reduce the damage.

Rhiannon said: "A glass of Pimms is undeniably delicious on a summer's day. To make it a little less sugary, try mixing it with sparkling water instead of fruit juice and lemonade.

"This will also help to keep you hydrated."

Pimms, however, isn't the only cocktail that contains more sugar than you think.

The Hospital Group also says that boozy favourites such as pina colada and pornstar martinis are way more sugary than a Krispy Kreme.

So an afternoon spent sipping a couple of Aperol Spritzes could send you way over that limit before you've even eaten a morsel of food.

If you really must drink a cocktail (and, let's face it, you really must) then a Margherita is the way to go.

A Margherita is not only the lowest calorie option, but also contains very little sugar.

There are just 0.3 grams of sugar in one Margarita - far less than the allowed 30g per day.

Dietician Helen Bond told us that "a Pina colada cocktail contains nearly 320 kcals in just one drink – nearly the same amount of calories as two Mars Bars (354 kcals)!"

In fact, Helen said that alcohol contains almost as many "empty" calories (seven per gram) as pure fat.

"Sugary drinks, along with alcohol, are among the worst offenders when it comes to racking up our intake of free sugars, with figures from the National Diet and Nutrition Survey revealing that booze accounts for nearly 10 per cent of adults’ free sugars intakes," she explained.

"And sweet alcohol favourites such as sweet wines and sherry, and liqueurs generally contain the most sugar.

"Just a glass of homemade mulled wine can contain over eight teaspoons of sugar - and 100 per cent of your daily maximum free sugar allowance."

Consuming excessive amounts of sugar comes with all sorts of health risks linked to obesity.

Being overweight can heighten your risk of 11 types of cancer, as well as type 2 diabetes, which occurs when the pancreas is unable to produce enough insulin to maintain a normal blood glucose level.

It also heightens your risk of developing complications such as heart attack and stroke.

Studies have also linked excessive sugar intake to Alzheimer's disease later in life.

Helen's tip?

"If you are going to drink cocktails, add ice to dilute them, refill your cocktail glass with sparkling water or a lower-calorie soft drink, and also look out for ‘mocktails’ - non-alcoholic tipples you’ll actually like, to make booze free nights more fun."

But if you do want a proper cocktail, you're probably best off opting for a Margarita.

Or, failing that, just a shot of tequila (on your own head be it!).
