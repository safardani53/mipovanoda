Kim Kardashian fans slam 'cash-strapped' Blac Chyna for promoting 'toxic' weight loss ad after star

Publish date: 2024-07-01

BLAC Chyna has been slammed for promoting a "toxic" weight loss ad after begging fans for money.

The 33-year-old asked fans to help her raise $400,000 so she could appeal her court defeat against the Kardashians earlier this week.

And on Thursday, the cash-strapped star turned to Instagram to bring in some revenue.

Chyna shared a TikTok of a woman claiming she went from 210lbs to 186lbs in a "few months" by using a weight loss cream.

Rob Kardashian's ex captioned it: "Get You Those Results You Been Wanting Forever!!

"@KO__Elixir Creams Are For Every Part Of Your Body Will give you Results That Will Have You Coming Back."

Chyna's followers flooded the comments with their outrage, slamming her for peddling products that "don't work".

One raged: "This is TOXIC!!! Please don’t promote this s**t Chyna.

"Women are going to buy this cream in the hopes of losing weight, and when they don’t. Then what?"

Another added: "This the kind of stuff that's going to get you sued! Stop promoting crap that you KNOW don't work."

A third user wrote: "Posting stuff like this when you’re desperate for money."

Earlier this week, Chyna suffered a major loss in court, not receiving any money in her defamation lawsuit against the Kardashians.

Chyna, who shares daughter Dream, five, with her ex Rob,  sued Kim, Khloe, as well as Kylie and Kris Jenner, for defamation and alleged interference with her E! contract.

CHYNA'S FIGHT

She accused the famous family of getting the second season of her E! show Rob & Chyna canceled and of ruining her reality TV career.

After almost two days of deliberation, the court revealed the jury ruled that no damages would be awarded to the model.

Chyna sat in stunned silence after the shocking verdict was read, according to a Sun reporter who was in court, while the defendants skipped out as they were attending the Met Gala in New York City on Monday.

GOFUNDME DREAM

One day after a verdict was reached, Chyna's mom Tokyo Toni begged fans for $400,000 so that her daughter can appeal the judge's decision.

Tokyo - whose real name is Shalana Hunter - started up a GoFundMe, titled Cheering for Blac Chyna's Appeal, which is asking for a whopping $400,000,

It reads: "Hi, I am Shalana Hunter- BKA Tokyo Toni. I would like to appeal to the courts.

"This GoFundMe fundraiser [will] help my daughter, Angela R. White - BKA Blac Chyna - financially in this endeavor to get justice."
