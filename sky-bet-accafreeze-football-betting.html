Sky Bet AccaFreeze: Choose the winning leg you want to freeze, and it will be settled as a winner!

Publish date: 2024-07-24

GET more control over you football accumulator with Sky Bet's new and innovative AccaFreeze!

AccaFreeze is new and exclusive to Sky Bet, and allows punters to freeze a winning score of your 5+ leg acca in-play. Simply select the winning leg you want to freeze, and it will be settled as a winner, even if it goes on to lose!

We've all been there. A team in your acca have taken the lead but the opposition are piling forward in search of an equaliser and you just know they'll snatch one.

Well, that's no longer an issue with AccaFreeze!

Simply open the Sky Bet app, head to 'My Bets' and if that team is currently winning - then you can click the option to freeze that leg of your 5+ leg acca and it will be settled as a winner.

Take the Champions League earlier this week. Red Star Belgrade were 20/1 to beat Manchester City and took a surprise lead just before the break.

With AccaFreeze, you could have frozen that leg of your acca and had it settled as a 20/1 winner!

Here's a quick guide on how to use AccaFreeze...

Sky Bet

  • Build an acca with 5 teams or more and add to your bet slip, including at least 1 team to win
  • Once a qualifying bet has been placed, you'll be able to see it in your 'My Bets'
  • Within 'My Bets', once one of the teams you've selected to win goes ahead in the match, the option to freeze will appear
  • Select the team you want to freeze the score for. You’ll get asked to ‘Confirm Selection to Freeze’. If you’re sure, freeze away! If you change your mind, just deselect the team to cancel it
  • Once a leg is frozen it will appear with a blue tick alongside your remaining legs. The score you froze will be shown in blue.
  • If your remaining selections in your acca go on to win, alongside your frozen leg, your bet will be settled as a winner. The odds will always be the same as what they were when you placed the bet!
  • Note, you only get one freeze per acca and a leg can’t be unfrozen once confirmed
