Jimmy's Farm run by Jamie Oliver's TV star pal RAM-RAIDED by coat thieves

Publish date: 2024-07-19

HUNDREDS of luxury country jackets worth thousands of pounds have been ram-raided from a high-profile TV star's farm.

Jimmy's Farm, run by Jamie Oliver's TV presenter pal Jimmy Doherty, was raided by a ruthless gang who smashed their way into the outlet near Wherstead, Suffolk.

The group made off with a haul of over 100 expensive Barbour and Schoffel-branded coats.

Police dashed to the scene just past 11pm last night after a burglar alarm was activated.

Cops found the highly-professional raiders had crashed a vehicle through a wooden fence and into the building.

The villains battered down a side wall, scattering bricks and masonry everywhere, before clinically plundering and ransacking the shop.

Detectives hunting the crooks are trying to establish if the raid was linked to six others across East Anglia between Tuesday night and Wednesday morning.

Devastated shop owner Martin Clarke said today: "We have been ramraided and have had a large number of items of clothing stolen.

"We have had people here all night since the alarm was raised."

Mr Clarke added: "It looks like they have used two cars, one to smash through the wall and the other to load up the clothing.

"Both cars were driven away."

High end clothing brand Barbour specialises in superior outdoor clothing for countryside pursuits.

The top company is best known for its jackets which cost up to £200 and above.

Schoffel is a similarly-priced countrywear brand founded in Germany 200 years ago.

Clarkes of Walsham is based at Walsham-le-Willows, west Suffolk.
