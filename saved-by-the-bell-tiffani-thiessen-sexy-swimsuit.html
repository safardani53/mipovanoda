TIFFANI Thiessen has shown off her swimsuit body in new photos.

The mother of two, known for her role in the original Saved by the Bell, was captured enjoying summer in new Instagram pics.

Tiffani, 49, played the popular Kelly Kapowski on the sitcom, beginning in 1989.

In the new social media shots, family members captured the now-mother of two as she spent time with her children in California.

In one post, Tiffani's husband Brady Smith shared a snap of himself, his wife, and their two children in a pool set above the beautiful landscape of the Sequoia National Park.

Tiffani smiled happily from between her kids, flaunting her curves in a navy blue swimsuit while keeping shaded from the sun with her hair tucked under a baseball cap.

FROM DRAMA TO MAMA

Tiffani Amber Thiessen was born on January 23, 1974, in Long Beach, California.

She appeared in her first commercial after family members encouraged her to take up acting when she was eight.

Her big break came when she was cast as cheerleader Kelly Kapowski on Saved by the Bell.

She would also star in Beverly Hills, 90210, and other television series during her career.

The TV and film star also hosted her own food-based television series called "Dinner at Tiffani's."

She is married to actor Brady Smith, and the couple shares two children.

Thiessen starred as Kelly Kapowski alongside Mark-Paul Gosselaar (Zack Morris), Mario Lopez (A.C. Slater), Dustin Diamond (Samual Screech Powers), and Lark Voorhies (Lisa Turtle).

The teen sitcom ran from 1989 to 1993, although there were two spin-off shows: The College Years and The New Class, which lasted until 2000.

She also starred in the 2020-2021 reboot of the hit show.

HEARTBREAKING LOSS

In 2021, Tiffani's co-star Dustin Diamond died aged 44 after a battle with cancer.

At the time, Dustin's close friend Dan Block confirmed the tragic news to The U.S. Sun.

Dan, who flew in to be by his bedside, told The U.S. Sun: "We thought he had five months or something. He was getting ready to go into the hospice today. 

"I was there, by his bed. He didn't say anything. He wasn't in a position to talk. He was laboring to breathe really bad. We're all so shocked."

The news was initially broken by TMZ, which shared one of the last photographs of Dustin in a hospital bed.

Shortly after the distressing news, Tiffani took to Twitter to comment on Dustin's passing.
