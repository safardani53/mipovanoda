Who won the St Leger at Doncaster? Steward's enquiry sees late change of placings

Publish date: 2024-06-29

THE St Leger at Doncaster is the final Classic of the season - and one of the biggest races all year.

It is the flagship race of the St Leger Festival and this year is run on Sunday, September 11.

The race is usually contested on the Saturday before.

But it was moved this year as all racing was cancelled on Saturday as a mark of respect after The Queen passed away peacefully aged 96.

Who won the St Leger?

ELDAR ELDAROV WINS IT!

A thoroughly convincing win for Eldar Eldarov and jockey David Egan - what a sensational ride!

He always had bags and bags of stamina about him and trainer Roger Varian singled out this race earlier this season.

Challenged by Derby runner-up Hoo Ya Mal down the outside, Eldar took his usual time to get going but, boy, when he did - wow!

Egan spoke brilliantly afterwards about how he dedicated the win to tragic Jack de Bromhead.

The 13-year-old son of Grand National-winning trainer Henry died last weekend in a pony racing accident.

Full credit to Frankie Dettori on Haskoy, who was in just her third race and in the famous Juddmonte colours and had come second before the placings were revised as below.
