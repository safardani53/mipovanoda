Leicester urge fans to 'be fearless' with new home kit for 2017/18 season... morning after 6-1 defea

Publish date: 2024-01-28

LEICESTER have urged their fans to "be fearless" in their new home kit - the morning after a 6-1 defeat to Spurs.

Craig Shakespeare's men were at the mercy of a rampant Harry Kane last night as he bagged four at the King Power.

But last season's Premier League champions attempted to make their supporters feel better with the release of their 2017/18 kit.

The traditional blue jersey produced by Puma makes a return and was modelled by Jamie Vardy, Wes Morgan and Kasper Schmeichel and Co.

Riyad Mahrez was noticeable in his absence for the promotional shots... adding fuel to the rumours he could be off in the summer.

A gold dotted pattern runs from the neck, across the shoulders and down the arm of the new shirt.

An adult's 2017/18 replica home shirt is available now on the official Leicester website for £50.

Mauricio Pochettino's men ran riot last night against last year's champs, who looked jaded and sloppy.

Heung-Min Son also bagged a brace for Spurs while Ben Chilwell was the only bright spark for the hosts.

He scored his first professional goal with a delicate finish to restore some pride.

Kane's heroics put him at the front of the race for the Premier League Golden Boot.

He sits on 26 league goals for this campaign, two ahead of Everton's Romelu Lukaku.
