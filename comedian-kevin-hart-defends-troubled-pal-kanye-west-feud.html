Comedian Kevin Hart defends pal Kanye West amid Kid Cudi feud as he joins Travis Scott at Super Bowl

Publish date: 2024-01-30

COMEDIAN Kevin Hart has defended pal Kanye West as he publicly feuds with Kid Cudi and Billie Eilish amid his messy divorce from Kim Kardashian.

The stand-up star, who has been friends with the 44-year-old rapper for many years, feels people need to accept Ye for who he is or ignore his online meltdowns.

Kanye has accused Cudi of betrayal and dropped him from his upcoming Donda 2 album for being pals with ex Kim's new man, SNL star Pete Davidson.

He has also lashed out at singer Billie, 20, insisting she needs to apologize to Travis Scott after an onstage comment seemingly about the Astroworld tragedy.

Speaking exclusively to The Sun, pal Kevin said: "Look, Kanye's Kanye, so it's drama to some but it's not drama to him.

"He's being himself and people need to either accept it or move on.”

When quizzed specifically about Ye's feud with Billie Eilish, he laughed: "I don't know. I'm 42, that stuff doesn't concern me."

Kevin had his say at Michael Rubin's Fanatics Super Bowl Party in Culver City on Saturday afternoon before the Los Angeles Rams face off with the Cincinnati Bengals.

He joined Ye's pal Travis Scott, Russell Wilson and wife Ciara, Shaquille O'Neal, Quavo, Meek Mill, and Emily Ratajkowski, who were among a number of stars at the lavish bash.

The invite-only soiree is now the hottest ticket in town ahead of the big game, with other guests including Ryan & Deacon Phillippe, The Chainsmokers, Nikki & Brie Bella, Tinashe, Robert Kraft, and A$AP Ferg.

Kevin and fellow partygoers watched performers Megan Thee Stallion and Doja Cat, while rapper Lil Baby missed his planned set following Friday night's shooting involving friend Kodak Black.

Publicity-shy Travis, who left girlfriend Kylie Jenner at home with their newborn son Wolf, was seen keeping a very low profile in the VIP area after skipping the red carpet.

Kevin said he was focusing on a positive weekend, telling The Sun: "I'm very happy to be here, Rubin is a good friend of mine, good people attract good energy."

He added: "I like the Bengals, hopefully they do it [win the Super Bowl], I'm just an underdog guy."

Meanwhile, Kid Cudi has responded to Kanye and trashed him online as he jumped in the comments section and claimed that he did not want to "be on your album, you f**king dinosaur."

Cudi added: "Everyone knows I've been the best thing about your albums since I met you."

He added: "I'm a pray for you brother," with a peace sign emoji.

CUDI FIRED BACK

Cudi, however, was not done with his rant and dropped another diss on Twitter.

He tweeted: "We talked weeks ago about this."

The music star continued: "You're whack for flipping the script and posting this lie just for a look on the internet."

Before Cudi said his farewell, he claimed that Kanye "ain’t no friend."

According to TMZ, SNL star Pete, 28, and Cudi have reportedly been buddies for years.

The two had even previously gone to an after-party event during the Met Gala fashion event back in September 2021.

The online beef comes after Kanye posted a grab from an article that alleged Billie Eilish had dissed Travis Scott over the Astroworld tragedy.

Kanye demanded that the young singer apologize and wrote in all caps: "COME ON BILLIE. WE LOVE YOU. PLEASE APOLOGIZE TO TRAV AND TO THE FAMILIES OF THE PEOPLE WHO LOST THEIR LIVES."

He continued: "NO ONE INTENDED THIS TO HAPPEN. TRAV DIDN’T HAVE ANY IDEA OF WHAT WAS HAPPENING WHEN HE WAS ON STAGE. AND WAS VERY HURT BY WHAT HAPPENED."
