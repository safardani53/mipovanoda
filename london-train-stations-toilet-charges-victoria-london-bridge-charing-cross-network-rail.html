London train station toilet charges at Victoria, London Bridge and Charing Cross scrapped by Network

Publish date: 2024-01-30

A CONTROVERSIAL 50p charge to use toilets at train stations will be scrapped to the delight of busting passengers.

The charge will be ditched at Victoria, London Bridge and Charing Cross stations - with the fee having brought in more than £6.4 million from Victoria alone over the past nine years.

Network Rail said it had re-evaluated its customer service to make the change as of Thursday.

A spokesman said: "Network Rail South East Route, which manages London Victoria, London Bridge, Charing Cross and Cannon Street, has stopped charging passengers to use the toilets at Victoria and Charing Cross.

"This brings those stations into line with London Bridge, which has not charged since the new concourse opened, and Cannon Street, which has not charged for some years."

The spokesperson added: "It will take us some time to assess the impact of that policy with regard to cleaning and security, and the costs involved in keeping standards as high as our expectations, so we are keeping an open mind.

"However, we do recognise that good customer service starts from the minute people walk into our buildings and we want them to feel welcome."

They previously explained the 50p charge had been higher than at other stations due to Victoria's toilets recently being refurbished.

Network Rail said that any profit made went towards the upkeep of a station.

Figures dating back to 2007 from Network Rail, show that the firm has raked in more than £37.5 million from passengers paying 50p, 40p or 30p at the 12 stations it owns across the UK where there have been charges for using the toilets over that time.

Victoria station made the most money - raking in more than £6,400,000.

Euston has made Network Rail the second most amount of money over the nine-year period, with £4,900,000, and King's Cross ranked third - £3,500,000.

Passengers paying to use facilities at other Network Rail-run stations across the UK pay 30p a pop - apart from at Glasgow Central and Leeds where the tariff is 40p.

During that 12-month period passengers paid £281,302 to use the toilets at Victoria but in the following financial year this figure dropped sharply to £69,781.25.

However, in the 2009/2010 financial year the amount of money received by Network Rail rocketed by more than 1,000 per cent as passengers paid £789,580.45 to use the toilets at Victoria.

The latest set of data released by Network Rail last month showed a continuing rise each financial year at Victoria and even smashed the £1 million mark - a figure which no other station has ever come close to.

Explaining the price rise to 50p, a Network Rail spokesman said: "Victoria station toilets have undergone a huge refurbishment recently and now offer top-of-the-range facilities including a full-time toilet attendant and showers.

"The price to use them has increased to reflect these changes and is on a par with other local amenities.

"This change has seen increased takings which are used to cover costs and are invested elsewhere in the station as Network Rail is a public company that ploughs any operating surplus back into the railway."
