Ingrid Bergman is a Swedish actress who has appeared in many European and American films, TV series and dramas. With a career spanning five decades, she is often considered one of the most influential screen figures in film history. She has won many awards, including three Academy Awards, two Primetime Emmy Awards, a Tony Award, four Golden Globe Awards, and a BAFTA Award. Born in Stockholm to a Swedish father and a German mother, Bergman began her acting career in Swedish and German films. Its introduction to American audiences is in the English remake of Intermezzo. Known for her sheer natural beauty, she performed in Casablanca as Ilsa Lund, her most famous role, opposite Humphrey Bogart's Rick Blaine.
